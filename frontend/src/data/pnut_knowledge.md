# บทที่ 1: Cardiovascular Disorder

---

## 1.1 Chest Pain — อาการเจ็บหน้าอก

### DDx Algorithm:
```
Chest pain
├── Cardiac
│   ├── Ischemic
│   │   ├── ACS
│   │   └── Stable angina
│   └── Non-Ischemic
│       ├── Endocardium → Severe AS
│       ├── Myocardium → HOCM
│       └── Pericardium → Pericarditis
└── Non-Cardiac
    ├── Great vessel → Aortic dissection (highlighted)
    ├── Pulmonary
    │   ├── Pleura: Pneumothorax, Pneumonia, Empyema thoracis
    │   └── Vascular: Pulmonary embolism (highlighted)
    ├── GI
    │   ├── Esophagus: GERD, Mallory-Weiss tear, Boerhaave syndrome (highlighted)
    │   ├── Stomach: Dyspepsia, PU perforate
    │   └── Pancreatitis
    ├── Musculoskeletal → Costochondritis, Rib fracture
    └── Skin → Herpes zoster
```

### Key Points:
- Highlighted dangerous causes: Aortic dissection, Pulmonary embolism, Boerhaave syndrome
- Must distinguish cardiac vs non-cardiac first

## 1.2 Palpitation — ใจสั่น

### DDx Algorithm:
```
Palpitation
├── Organic
│   ├── Cardiac
│   │   ├── Arrhythmia
│   │   │   ├── Premature beat: PAC, PVC
│   │   │   ├── Irregular: AF, MAT
│   │   │   ├── Tachyarrhythmia: SVT, VT
│   │   │   └── Bradyarrhythmia: AV block, SSS
│   │   └── Non-Arrhythmia
│   │       ├── Structural: ASD, VSD, VHD, Cardiomyopathy
│   │       └── Functional: CHF, MI
│   └── Non-Cardiac
│       ├── High output state: Anemia, fever, pregnancy, Stress/exercise, Volume depletion
│       ├── Metabolic: Hypoglycemia, Thyrotoxicosis, Pheochromocytoma, K/Mg/Ca low
│       └── Drug & Med: Coffee/smoke/alcohol, Cocaine/amphetamine, B2-agonist, Ephedrine
└── Psychiatric → Panic attack, Anxiety
```

## 1.3 Syncope — หมดสติชั่วคราว

### DDx Algorithm:
- Syncope Triad: 1) Transient loss of consciousness, 2) Loss of postural tone, 3) Brief episodes with spontaneous recovery

```
Syncope
├── Reflex syncope
│   ├── Vasovagal — Mediated by: Sneeze, Emotion/Pain/Fear/Phobia, Micturition, Post exercise
│   ├── Situational
│   └── Carotid sinus
├── Orthostatic hypotension
│   ├── Drug-induced: Alcohol, Vasodilator, Diuretic, Antidepressant, Phenothiazine
│   └── Autonomic failure
│       ├── Primary: Pure ANS failure
│       └── Secondary: DM, Amyloid, Uremia, Cord injury
├── Cardiac syncope
│   ├── Non-cardiac: Massive PE, Aortic dissection, PHT
│   └── Cardiac
│       ├── Arrhythmia
│       │   ├── Brady: SSS, AV block, VT
│       │   └── Tachy: SVT, VT
│       └── Structural
│           ├── Endocardial: VHD, Atrial myxoma
│           ├── Myocardium: HOCM, MI
│           └── Pericardium: Cardiac tamponade
└── Volume depletion: Bleed, Diarrhea
```

### Syncope Approach:
- Risk assessment: Admit or Observe or Discharge?
- High risk: Congestive heart failure, structural heart disease, Hct <30, SBP <90, Shortness of breath, Age >45

## 1.4 Claudication — อาการปวดขาเวลาเดิน

### DDx Algorithm:
```
Claudication
├── Vascular claudication
│   ├── Cause: Sign of 6P (Pain, Pallor, Polycythemia, Paresthesia, Paralysis, Pulseless)
│   ├── Abnormal vessel: Atherosclerosis, Vasculitis, Takayasu arteritis, Giant cell arteritis, Thromboangiitis obliterans (Buerger's disease)
│   ├── Thrombophilia: Thrombosis, Embolic (cardiac origin, artery to artery)
│   └── Vasospasm: Ergotamine
└── Neurogenic claudication
    ├── Cause: Paresthesia of leg, weakness of leg
    ├── PE: เดินแล้วเจ็บ หยุดนั่งยืด แล้วหายเจ็บ ต้องก้มตัวเดินไปข้างหน้า
    └── L/S radiculopathy: Spinal stenosis, Herniated disc, Leriche's syndrome, Iliac/femoral/popliteal stenosis
```

---

## 1.5 Congestive Heart Failure and Cardiomyopathy — ภาวะหัวใจล้มเหลว

### DDx Algorithm:
```
CHF
├── Etiology
│   ├── Valvular heart dz: Stenosis (AS, MS), Regurg (AR, MR), Congenital
│   ├── Cardiomyopathy
│   │   ├── DCM (Dilated): Familial, Myocarditis, Alcohol, Nutritional/Endocrine, Preg, Tachycardia-induced
│   │   ├── HCM (Hypertrophic): Familial, Obesity, Athletic, Amyloid
│   │   ├── RCM (Restrictive): Familial, Amyloidosis, Scleroderma, Endomyocardial fibrosis, Anthracyclines
│   │   └── ARVC (Arrhythmogenic Right Ventricular): Familial ARVC, Inflammation
│   └── Pericardial dz: Constrictive pericarditis, Pericardial effusion
├── Precipitating factor (Mnemonic: "DIE HEART")
│   D: Diet (high salt), Drug withdrawal/diuretic, Drug administration (NSAIDs)
│   I: Infection
│   E: Exercise, Embolism (= Myocardial infarction)
│   H: Hypertensive emergency
│   E: (above)
│   A: Anemia
│   R: Rheumatic (Valvular dz), Renal failure
│   T: Thyrotoxicosis
└── Exclude: Hypertensive CM, Ischemic CM, Valvular heart dz

Cardiomyopathy (Etiology)
├── Familial → Familial DCM
├── Nutrition/Toxin
│   ├── Nutritional: Thiamine def, L-carnitine def
│   ├── Metabolic (Endocrine): Obesity, Diabetic, Thyroid, GH
│   └── Toxic: Alcohol, Cocaine/Amphetamine, Chemotherapy, Doxorubicin, Trastuzumab, High dose CY, Taxoid/mitomycin-C, 5-FU/IFN
├── Inflammation
│   ├── Infection: Myocarditis, AIDS, Chagas dz
│   └── Non-Infection: Hypersensitivity, CNT, RA, Scleroderma
├── Deposition: Amyloidosis, Fe overload, Tachycardia-induced CM
└── Miscellaneous: Idiopathic DCM, Peripartum CM, Takotsubo, Cardiac sarcoidosis
```

## 1.6 Hypertension in the Young — ความดันสูงในคนอายุน้อย

### DDx Algorithm:
- SBP >= 140 or DBP >= 90 mmHg ในผู้ป่วยที่มีอายุน้อยกว่า 35 ปี
- Essential HT ยังคง most common

```
Consider Secondary HT
├── CVS: Coarctation of aorta, Takayasu arteritis
├── Renal
│   ├── Renovascular HT: Atherosclerosis, Fibromuscular dysplasia, PAN
│   ├── Abnormal kidney: CKD, PCKD, Horseshoe
│   └── Glomerulonephritis
├── Endocrine
│   ├── Adrenal: Hyperaldosteronism, Pheochromocytoma
│   ├── Pituitary: Cushing, Acromegaly
│   └── Thyrotoxicosis
└── Miscellaneous
    ├── OSA
    ├── Drug: NSAIDs, Decongestant, OCP/steroid, Cocaine/TCA
    └── Genetic: Neurofibromatosis, Von-Hippel-Lindau
```

## 1.7 Pericardial Effusion — น้ำในเยื่อหุ้มหัวใจ

### Key Signs:
- No specific s/s until tamponade
- Cardiac tamponade: Pulsus paradoxus SBP > 20 mmHg
- Beck triad: JVP distension, BP drop, distant heart sound
- EKG: electrical alternan or low voltage EKG

### DDx Algorithm:
```
Pericardial effusion
├── Infection
│   ├── Virus: Coxsackie, Adeno, Echo
│   ├── Bacteria: Staph, Strep
│   ├── High bact: Nocardia, Actino
│   ├── Ricketsia, Borrelia
│   ├── Mycobacteria
│   └── Fungus: Histo, Coccidio
└── Non-Infection
    ├── Trauma → Blunt injury
    └── Non-Trauma
        ├── Tumor: Lymphoma, Metastatic CA (Lung, Breast)
        ├── CNT: SLE, Vasculitis, PAN, Wegener
        ├── Metabolic: Uremia, Hypothyroid
        ├── Drug & toxin: INH, Hydralazine, Procainamide
        └── CVS: Dressler's syndrome, Dissecting aortic aneurysm
```

# บทที่ 2: Respiratory Disorder

---

## 2.1 Dyspnea and Positional Related Dyspnea — หอบเหนื่อย

### DDx Algorithm:
```
Dyspnea
├── Respiratory
│   ├── Airway: COPD, Asthma
│   ├── Parenchyma: Infection, Pneumonia, PCR/TB/other, CA (1st or Metas), ILD
│   ├── Pleura: Pneumothorax, Hemothorax, Pleural effusion
│   └── Vascular: PE, PHT
├── Cardio: CHF, DKA
├── Metabolic: Anemia, Acidosis, Thyrotoxicosis
├── Neuromuscular: Diaphragm paralysis, R/S m.weak/Hypoph, NMJ (MG), PMD (GBS), ALS
├── Psychi: In drug, Anxiety, Panic
└── Other: Obesity

Positional Related Dyspnea:
1. Orthopnea (นอนหงายแล้วเหนื่อย):
   - Cardio: CHF, AS, Airway: Asthma, Pleural effusion, NMJ, Abdominal: Massive ascites
2. Trepopnea (ตะแคงข้างนึงเหนื่อย):
   - Unilateral lung disease, CHF (dup.DCM), Mediastinal tumor, Endobronchial tumor
3. Platypnea-Orthodeoxia (นั่งเหนื่อย):
   - Intracardiac shunt: PFO or ASD
   - Pulmonary: Pulmonary embolism, Pericardial effusion, Pneumonectomy
   - Intrapulmonary shunt
   - Hepatopulmonary syndrome
```

---

## 2.2 Cough — ไอ

### DDx Algorithm:
- Duration: Acute (<3 weeks), Subacute (3-8 weeks), Chronic (>8 weeks)

```
Cough
├── Respiratory
│   ├── Airway: COPD/Asthma, Bronchitis, Airway irritant (smoke/dust), Foreign body, Upper airway cough syndrome
│   └── Parenchyma: Infection (Pneumonia, TB), CA (1st or Metas), ILD, Bronchiectasis
└── Other
    ├── Cardio: CHF
    ├── GI: GERD
    ├── ENT: Allergic rhinitis, Sinusitis, Ear foreign body, Laryngopharyngeal reflux
    ├── Drug: ACEI
    └── Other: Mediastinal mass, Psychi

Duration Table:
| Acute        | Subacute              | Chronic                          |
| URI          | Post infectious cough | ACEI, Asthma, GERD               |
| Acute decompensate HF |                  | Upper Airway Cough syndrome      |
| Exacerbate COPD/Asthma |               |                                   |
| LRTI         |                       |                                   |
```

---

## 2.3 Hemoptysis — ไอเป็นเลือด

### DDx Algorithm:
- ต้องแยก True hemoptysis vs Pseudohemoptysis (Epistaxis, Hematemesis)
- Massive hemoptysis: เลือดออกเป็นลิ่มสด > 200 ml/ครั้ง หรือ > 600 ml/24h

```
True hemoptysis
├── Airway
│   ├── Infection: Bronchitis, Bronchiectasis
│   └── Tumor: Bronchogenic CA
├── Parenchyma
│   ├── Infection: Lung abscess, Pneumonia
│   └── Tumor: Primary or Metas, Trauma (Lung contusion), Small vessel (Good Pasture's)
├── Vascular: Pulmonary embolism, AVM
└── Miscellaneous: Pulmonary endometriosis, Idiopathic pulmonary hemosiderosis
```

---

## 2.4 Cyanosis — เขียว

### DDx Algorithm:
```
Cyanosis
├── Peripheral cyanosis (skin only, spare mucous membrane)
│   ├── Reduce cardiac output
│   ├── Cold exposure
│   ├── Venous obstruction
│   └── Arterial obstruction: PAD, Emboli
└── Central cyanosis (involve mucous membrane and skin)
    ├── Low O2 sat
    │   ├── RS
    │   │   ├── Parenchyma: Alveolar hypoventilation, V/Q mismatch, Diffusion defect
    │   │   └── Vascular: Pulmonary AVF, PE
    │   ├── Low FiO2
    │   └── Cardio → Cyanotic heart dz "5T+2E"
    │       ├── 5T: TOF, TGV, Truncus Arteriosus, Tricuspid atresia, TAPVR
    │       └── 2E: Ebstein anomaly, Eisenmenger in ASD/VSD/PDA
    └── Hb abnormal
        ├── Methemoglobinemia
        └── Sulfhemoglobinemia
```

---

## 2.5 Clubbing of Finger — นิ้วปุ้ม

### DDx Algorithm:
- 5 signs: Floating nail sign, Profile sign (earliest), Drummond's sign, Chromatic-like of terminal phalanx, Diameter of nail bed > DIP

```
Clubbing
├── Primary (Familial or idiopathic)
└── Secondary
    ├── Cardio: Cyanotic heart disease, Infective endocarditis
    ├── Pulmonary: Neoplasm, Bronchial carcinoma, Lung metastasis, Mesothelioma, Chronic lung disease, Bronchiectasis, Empyema, IPF, Infection (Lung abscess, Empyema)
    ├── GI: Cirrhosis, IBD, Crohn's, Ulcerative colitis, Celiac disease, Esophageal CA/achalasia
    └── Miscellaneous: Endocrine (Thyrotoxicosis, POEMS), Hematologic (PV with Hypoxia, Methemoglobinemia, Lymphoma), Autoimmune (PAN, SLE)
```

---

## 2.6 Pleural Effusion — น้ำในเยื่อหุ้มปอด

### DDx Algorithm:
- Light's criteria: Exudate if Prot Pleura/Serum > 0.5, LDH Pleura/Serum > 0.6, LDH pleura > 2/3 x ULN

```
Pleural effusion
├── Exudate (turbid, odor)
│   ├── Malignancy: Metastatic CA, Mesothelioma, Lymphoma
│   ├── Infection: Empyema thoracis, Parapneumonic, TB pleuritis, Viral/Fungus
│   ├── CNT: RA, SLE, CSS, WG
│   ├── Drug: Methotrexate, Amiodarone
│   ├── GI: Pancreatitis
│   └── Other: Parasites, Chylothorax
└── Transudate (clear, odorless, not met Light's criteria)
    ├── Clinical: CHF, Renal failure, Cirrhosis
    ├── CVS: CHF, SVC syndrome
    └── Renal: NS, Peritoneal dialysis, Urinothorax, Myxedema

Clinical Clues Table:
| Finding                              | Possible Cause                                    |
| FC change, orthopnea, PND            | Congestive heart failure                          |
| Signs of chronic liver dz and ascites | Cirrhosis with hepatic hydrothorax               |
| Foamy urine, generalized edema       | Nephrotic syndrome                                |
| Malignancy risk (เบื่ออาหาร น้ำหนักลดลง) | Malignant pleural effusion, lymphoma, mesothelioma |
| Fever, cough, dyspnea                | Empyema thoracis, parapneumonic effusion          |
| Multisystem involvement              | Connective tissue disease                         |
| ได้รับยา MTX, Ergot, Amiodarone      | Drug induced                                      |
| DVT แล้วมี dyspnea/chest pain       | Pulmonary embolism                                |
```

# บทที่ 3: Gastrointestinal Disorder

---

## 3.1 Jaundice — ดีซ่าน

### DDx Algorithm:
- Yellowish of tissue, TB > 3 mg/dl → icteric sclera
- แบ่ง 3 กลุ่ม: Hemolytic, Hepatocellular, Obstructive jaundice

```
Jaundice
├── Hemolytic jaundice
│   ├── Hx: Anemia, Sx Fatigue, Drug Hx, Dark urine
│   ├── PE: Pale, Splenomegaly
│   ├── LFT: Indirect bilirubin สูง
│   ├── Intrinsic Factor: Sph, HE, Elliptocytes, Enz G6PD deficiency, Hb: Thalassemia
│   └── Extrinsic Factor: MAHA, AHA, Malaria
├── Hepatocellular jaundice
│   ├── Hx: Flu Sx, Drug Hx, Alcohol, Blood transfusion, 3L
│   ├── LFT: Isolated bilirubin สูง or AST/ALT สูง and bilirubin สูง
│   ├── Isolated hyperbilirubinemia
│   │   ├── Direct (DB > 15%): Dubin-Johnson syn, Rotor's syndrome
│   │   └── Indirect (DB < 15%): Increase production (ภาวะเม็ดเลือดแดงแตก), Hemolytic anemia, Drug (Rifam), Decrease conjugation (Gilbert, Crigler-Najjar), Drug induced
│   ├── Acute liver injury: Viral (HBV, HAV, EBV, CMV, Herpes), Non-viral (Lepto, Scrub), Alcohol, Drug (Paracetamol, INH), Toxin (Amanita, Herb), Fatty liver of pregnancy
│   └── Chronic liver injury → Liver cirrhosis
│       ├── Alcohol, Virus (HBV/HCV), Cardiac (Wilson), Hemochromatosis
│       ├── Biliary cirrhosis: PBC, PSC
│       └── Other: NASH, Autoimmune cholangiopathy, Cryptogenic
└── Obstructive jaundice
    ├── Hx: Abd pain, Itching, Pale stool, Dark urine
    ├── PE: S mass at abdomen, LFT: ALP สูง
    ├── Choledocholithiasis, Bile duct dz
    ├── Sclerosing cholangitis, Post surg stenosis
    ├── AIDS cholangiopathy
    ├── Cholangiocarcinoma
    ├── Extrinsic compression
    └── Periampullary CA: 1) CA head of pancreas, 2) CA Ampulla of Vater, 3) CA 2nd part duodenum, 4) Cholangiocarcinoma
        - Lymphoma, HCC with cirrhosis
```

## 3.2 Dysphagia — กลืนลำบาก

### DDx Algorithm:
- ต้อง exclude odynophagia ก่อน

```
Dysphagia
├── Oropharyngeal (เป็นช่วงต้นการกลืน เด่นสำลัก ไอ)
│   ├── Structural
│   │   ├── Intrinsic narrowing: Congenital web, Plummer-Vinson, Zenker diverticulum, Neoplasm
│   │   └── Extrinsic compression: Goiter, Osteophyte/LN
│   └── Propulsive
│       ├── Neuro
│       │   ├── CNS: Cortex (Stroke), Parkinson, Brainstem lesion
│       │   └── PNS: GBS, ALS, MS
│       └── Muscular
│           ├── NMJ: MG, Botulism
│           └── Muscle: PM, OPMD
└── Esophageal (เด่นกลืนติด)
    ├── Structural (ติดของแข็ง)
    │   ├── Progressive: Neoplasm
    │   ├── Intermittent: Esophageal web, Schatzki ring
    │   └── Variable: Eosinophilic esophagitis, Peptic stricture, Hiatal hernia
    └── Propulsive (ติดของเหลว)
        ├── GERD with peristalsis weakness
        ├── Achalasia
        ├── DES
        └── Scleroderma
```

## 3.3 Vomiting — อาเจียน

### DDx Algorithm:
```
Vomiting
├── Intracranial and Pericranial
│   ├── Brain → Increase ICP "น้ำ-เลือด-เนื้อ-หนอง"
│   │   น้ำ: Hydrocephalus
│   │   เลือด: ICH, SDH, SAH
│   │   เนื้อ: Tumor
│   │   หนอง: Meningitis, Encephalitis
│   │   Idiopathic: Pseudotumor cerebri
│   ├── Vestibular: Labyrinthitis, Motion sickness
│   └── Eye: Glaucoma
└── Extracranial
    ├── GI
    │   ├── Functional: IBS, Gastroparesis
    │   └── Organic "Obstruct-Inflam"
    │       ├── Obstruction: Gastric outlet, Small bowel
    │       └── Inflammation
    │           ├── Stomach: PU, Food poisoning
    │           ├── Hep-Bili: Hepatitis, Cholecystitis
    │           ├── Pancreas: Pancreatitis
    │           └── Bowel: AGE, Peritonitis, Appendicitis
    ├── GU: Pyelonephritis, Stone, PID, Pregnancy
    ├── Metabolic
    │   ├── Endo: AI, DKA
    │   └── Nephro: Uremia, Na low
    └── Drug & toxin: Chemo/Opioid, NSAID/ATB, Organophosphate
```

## 3.4 Hiccup — สะอึก

### DDx Algorithm:
- Transient: 2-3 minutes, Benign: Usually < 1 Hr, Intractable: > 1 Hr *ต้องหาสาเหตุ*

```
Hiccup (Intractable)
├── Diaphragm irritation: Subphrenic abscess, Peritonitis
├── Mediastinum irritation: Mediastinitis, Aortic aneurysm
├── CNS: Vascular (Ischemic, ICH), Infection (Meningitis, Encephalitis), Tumor, Autoimmune (MS)
├── Metabolic: Uremia, Electrolyte (Na/Ca/K low), Hyperglycemia
└── Drug & toxin: Alcohol, BZD, Barbiturate, Dexamethasone
- ถ้าหาภาวะ organic ออกไปได้ ก็อาจจะเป็นภาวะ 'Psychogenic': Stress, Anxiety, Malingering
```

## 3.5 Upper Gastrointestinal Hemorrhage — เลือดออกทางเดินอาหารส่วนบน

### DDx Algorithm:
- Hx: Hematemesis, coffee ground emesis
- Stool: Melena, Maroon (active UGIB may → intravascular color)

```
Upper GI bleeding
├── ตรวจเบื้องต้น: H/C then liver dz or portal HT จาก history
├── Variceal bleeding (Signs of portal HT: caput medusae)
│   ├── Esophageal varices
│   ├── Gastric varices
│   ├── Duodenal varices
│   └── Portal hypertensive gastropathy
└── Non-Variceal bleeding
    ├── Ulcerative: Peptic ulcer disease (NSAIDs, ASA, H. pylori, Zollinger-Ellison syndrome)
    ├── Erosive: Esophagitis
    ├── Traumatic/Post-Surg: Mallory-Weiss tear, Aortoenteric fistula, Hemobilia
    ├── Tumor: Adenocarcinoma, GIST, Lymphoma
    ├── Vascular malformation: Dieulafoy's lesion, Vascular lesion, Hereditary hemorrhagic telangiectasia (Osler-Weber-Rendu), Gastric antral vascular ectasia
    └── Don't forget: Hemoptysis (swallowed blood)
```

---

## 3.6 Lower Gastrointestinal Hemorrhage — เลือดออกทางเดินอาหารส่วนล่าง

### DDx Algorithm:
- Hx: Hematochezia, Stool: Fresh blood per rectum, Maroon

```
Lower GI bleeding
├── Exclude: systemic bleeding
├── Look for anogenital cause: Hemorrhoid, Rectal ulcer
├── Diverticular disease
├── Angiodysplasia
├── Colitis: Infection, Ischemic colitis, IBD (Crohn, UC), Radiation
├── Neoplasm: Polyp, Carcinoma
└── Don't forget UGI bleeding and small intestine bleeding
```

## 3.7 Acute Diarrhea — ท้องเสียเฉียบพลัน

### DDx Algorithm:
- < 2 wk: Acute, 2-4 wk: Persistent, > 4 wk: Chronic

```
Acute diarrhea
├── Infection (> 90%)
│   ├── Watery diarrhea (Non-inflammatory)
│   │   ├── Vomiting dominant → Disease caused by Toxin: Food poisoning (Norovirus, Rotavirus)
│   │   └── Diarrhea/Abd pain dominant → Disease caused by Organism
│   │       ├── Virus: Norovirus, Rotavirus
│   │       ├── Bacteria: ETEC, Vibrio, Yersinia/Aeromonas, Campylobacter, C. difficile
│   │       └── Protozoa: Giardia lamblia, Cryptosporidium, Cyclospora
│   └── Mucous bloody diarrhea (Inflammatory)
│       ├── Virus: Cytoplasm
│       ├── Bacteria: EHEC, C. difficile, E. parahaemolyticus
│       ├── Mucosal invasion: EEC, Shigella, Salmonella, Aeromonas/Yersinia
│       └── Protozoa: E. Histolytica
└── Non-Infection (< 10%)
    ├── Drug: Colchicine, laxative
    ├── Toxin: Organophosphate, Mushroom, Seafood/shellfish
    ├── Food: Anaphylaxis
    ├── Inflam: Diverticulitis, IBD
    ├── Radiation
    └── Infant: Ischemic colitis
```

## 4.3 Stepwise Approach to Azotemia — แนวทางดูแล BUN/Cr สูง

### DDx Algorithm:
```
Azotemia = Elevation of BUN and Cr
├── Step 1: Exclude out of proportion BUN or Cr elevation
│   ├── Out of proportion BUN สูง
│   │   1. Urea generation สูง: Catabolic state, High protein intake, Aminoacid infusion, GI bleeding
│   │   2. Steroid therapy or Tetracycline
│   └── Out of proportion Cr สูง
│       1. Creatinine generation สูง: Rhabdomyolysis
│       2. Creatinine secretion ลด: Cimetidine, Trimethoprim
│       3. Jaffe reaction: Ketone, Cephalosporin
├── Step 2: แยก CKD vs Acute kidney injury
│   ├── AKI definition: SCr >= 0.3 mg/dl in 48 hrs, SCr >= 1.5 times baseline in 7 days, Urine < 0.5 ml/kg/hr for 6 hours
│   └── CKD: abnormal kidney structure or function >= 3 months
│       Features: Elevation of Cr > 3 months, PE: uremic skin, Lab: anemia, Imaging: bilateral small kidney, X-ray: renal osteodystrophy
├── Step 3: แยก Pre-renal vs Intrinsic Renal vs Post-renal
│   ├── Pre-renal
│   │   ├── Hypovolemia
│   │   ├── Decrease effective circulatory volume: Liver failure, Cardiac failure
│   │   ├── Decrease cardiac output
│   │   └── Impair renal autoregulation: NSAIDs, ACEI/ARB, Cisplatin
│   ├── Intrinsic Renal (ดูต่อ)
│   └── Post-renal obstruction: Full bladder/Hydronephrosis → Urinary tract obstruction

Intrinsic renal causes of AKI:
├── Vessel
│   ├── Large vessel
│   │   ├── Renal artery: Emboli, dissection, Vasculitis
│   │   ├── Renal vein: Thrombosis
│   │   └── Abdominal compartment syndrome
│   └── Small vessel: GN/Vasculitis, TTP/HUS, DIC, Malignant HT, Atheroemboli, Calcineurin inhibitor
├── Glomerulus: Acute GN, RPGN
├── Tubule
│   ├── Ischemic ATN
│   ├── Nephrotoxic ATN
│   │   ├── Endogenous: Rhabdomyolysis, Hemolysis
│   │   └── Exogenous: Contrast, Aminoglycoside
│   └── Intratubular obstruction
│       ├── Endogenous: Myeloma protein, Uric acid, Cellular debris
│       └── Exogenous: Acyclovir, Methotrexate
└── Interstitium (Mnemonic "A+3I")
    ├── Allergic: Penicillin, Rifam
    ├── Infection: Pyelonephritis
    ├── Infiltration: Lymphoma
    └── Inflammation: Sjogren, Sepsis
```

## 6.7 Coagulopathy — ความผิดปกติการแข็งตัวของเลือด

### DDx Algorithm:
```
Coagulopathy
├── Isolated PT สูง
│   ├── Congenital: Factor VII deficiency
│   └── Acquired: Liver disease, DIC, Warfarin, FVX inhibitor

├── Isolated PTT สูง → Exclude Heparin → Bleeding or Clot?

│       ├── TT normal → Inhibitor (Factor 10,5,2), Deficiency (Factor 10, 5, 2 def), Liver disease, Vit K def/Warfarin, Amyloid/DIC
│       └── TT สูง → Exclude Heparin
│           ├── Congenital: Afibrinogenemia, Hypofibrinogenemia, Dysfibrinogenemia
```

## 9.2 Drop Attack and T-LOC — หมดสติชั่วครู่

### DDx Algorithm:
```

├── Without loss of consciousness (Presyncope)
│   ├── TIA: Vertebrobasilar artery, ACA
│   ├── 3rd ventricle/posterior fossa tumor
│   ├── Chiari malformation
│   └── Otolithic crisis (Vestibular drop attack in Meniere disease)

| Feature                | Syncope         | Seizure          |

| Automatisms            | Occasional      | Common           |
| Tongue biting, lateral | Rare            | Occasional       |
| Frothing/Incontinence  | Rare            | Common           |
| Confusion post         | Rare, <30 sec   | Common, several min |
| Diffuse myalgia        | Rare, brief     | Common, hours-days |
```

## 9.3 Stepwise Approach to Seizure and Epilepsy — ชัก

### DDx Algorithm:
```
Seizure = transient s/s of abnormal excessive hypersynchronous activity in brain
Epilepsy = >= 2 unprovoked seizure

Step 1: Exclude seizure-like symptoms (Syncope, Migraine, Sleep disorders, Paroxysmal Movement Disorders, Psychogenic Nonepileptic Seizures)
Step 2: Precipitating factor (provoke vs non provoke)
  - Neurologic disorder (ผิดปกติในหัว): Metabolic/Drug/toxin (ผิดปกติจากนอกหัว)
  - Other: Sleep deprivation, Menstrual period (Catamenial seizure), Flashing light (Pokemon seizure)
Step 3: New onset seizure vs Recurrent unprovoked seizure

New onset seizure
├── Neurologic disorder
│   ├── Stroke: Ischemic stroke, Hemorrhagic stroke
│   ├── Head trauma
│   ├── Vascular: Vascular malformation
│   ├── Infection: Encephalitis, Cysticercosis
│   └── Brain tumor
├── Systemic disorder
│   ├── Metabolic: Hypoglycemia, Na/Ca/Mg low, Uremia
│   ├── Medication: AED poor compliance, Drug interaction with AED, BZD withdrawal, Sympathomimetic drug
│   ├── Drug abuse and toxin: Alcohol intoxication/withdrawal, Drug withdrawal, Cocaine
│   └── Other: HT encephalopathy, Eclampsia

Recurrent unprovoked seizure (Epilepsy)
├── Epilepsy in adult
│   ├── Genetic: Idiopathic generalized epilepsy, Childhood absence epilepsy, Benign childhood with centrotemporal seizure
│   ├── Neurocutaneous: Tuberous sclerosis, Sturge-Weber syndrome, Neurofibromatosis
│   ├── Epileptic encephalopathies: Lennox-Gastaut syndrome
│   ├── Structural/Metabolic
│   │   ├── Mesial temporal lobe epilepsy
│   │   ├── Localized lesional epilepsy: Glioma, Cavernoma, Focal cortical dysplasia, Dysembryoplastic NeuroEpithelial Tumor (DNET)
│   │   └── Hemispheric epilepsy: Hemimegalocephaly, Rasmussen's encephalitis
│   └── Unknown cause (Cryptogenic): Post hypoxic encephalopathy, Post traumatic encephalopathy, Large AVM, Old stroke
```

## 9.5 Approach to Weakness — อ่อนแรง

### 9.5.1 Weakness Nomenclature:
- Quadriparesis: อ่อนแรงแขนและขา 4 ข้าง
- Paraparesis: อ่อนแรงแขนหรือขาทั้ง 2 ข้าง โดยที่เป็นเฉลี่ยคู่กัน
- Hemiparesis: อ่อนแรงแขนและขาซ้ายหรือขวา ข้างใดข้างหนึ่ง
- Monoparesis: อ่อนแรงแขนเดียวหรือขาเดียว
- Proximal muscle weakness: อ่อนแรงต้นแขน ต้นขา (หวีผม หิ้วกระเป๋า ขึ้นบันได)
- Distal muscle weakness: อ่อนแรงปลายมือ ปลายเท้า (กลัดกระดุม มือสั่น เดินลากเท้า)

### 9.5.2 Approach to Hemiparesis
```
Hemiparesis
├── Cerebrum
│   ├── Cortical lesion: Weakness + Cortical signs (Aphasia if hemispheric, Neglect if non-hemispheric)
│   └── Subcortical lesion: Weakness เฉพาะแขนขาครึ่งหนึ่ง ไม่มี cortical signs
├── Brainstem: Midrain (Oculomotor hemiplegia), Pons (Abducen hemiplegia), Medulla (Hypoglossal hemiplegia)
└── Etiology
    ├── Stroke (Embolic, Multiple sclerosis, Thrombosis, Hemorrhagic)
    ├── Demyelination (Multiple sclerosis, Glioma, Metastasis)
    └── Tumor

Lacunar infarction types:
| Pure motor hemiparesis            | Subcortical lesion in corona radiata, internal capsule, or pons |
| Pure hemisensory loss             | Contralateral ventroposterolateral nucleus of thalamus         |
| Ataxic hemiparesis                | Contralateral posterior limb of internal capsule               |
| Dysarthria-clumsy hand syndrome   | Basis pontis lesion                                            |
```

### 9.5.3 Approach to Paraparesis
```
Paraparesis
├── UMN lesion
│   ├── Cerebral cortex: Parasagittal lesion (ACA stroke, Cerebral venous thrombosis, Parasagittal meningioma)
│   └── Spinal cord → ดูต่อเรื่อง Spinal cord disorder
└── LMN lesion
    ├── Anterior horn cell: PE signs UMN+LMN (Hyperreflexia + m.atrophy) → ALS, Motor neuron disease
    ├── Conus medullaris: Tumor, Infiltrative disease, Spina bifida, Myelomeningocele
    ├── Cauda equina: Tumor (Meningioma), Infection (Arachnoiditis, Abscess), Trauma
    ├── Bilateral LS plexopathy: Malignancy, Radiation, Diabetic amyotrophy
    └── Polyneuropathy: GBS, Porphyria

Conus medullaris vs Cauda equina:
| Feature              | Conus medullaris        | Cauda equina                    |
| Weakness             | Symmetrical             | Asymmetrical                    |
| Sensory loss         | Pain, Temp (dissociated)| Pain, Temp, Touch               |
| Bowel-Bladder        | Early onset             | Late onset                      |
| Other                |                         | Radicular pain, Reflex loss     |
```

### 9.5.4 Approach to Proximal Muscle Weakness
```
Proximal muscle weakness
├── NMJ
│   ├── Pre-synaptic: Lambert-Eaton, Botulism, Snake venom
│   └── Post-synaptic: Myasthenia gravis
├── Myopathy
│   ├── Congenital: Nemaline, Central core, Centronuclear
│   ├── Muscular dystrophy: Dystrophin def (Becker, Duchenne, Emery Dreifuss, Limb-girdle, Fascioscapulohumeral)
│   ├── Metabolic: Glycogen storage, Lipid storage
│   ├── Inflammation: DM/PM (middle age), Inclusion body myositis (old age), Non-immune infection
│   ├── Drug & Toxin: Statin/Colchicine, Chloroquine, Steroid, Tetradecylstin
│   └── Elyte + Endo: Electrolyte (Hypokal K, Hypo/hyperCa, K/Mg low), Endocrine (Thyroid, PTH)
└── Polyneuropathy
    ├── GBS, CIDP, Porphyria
```

### 9.5.5 Approach to Distal Muscle Weakness
```
Distal muscle weakness
├── Peripheral neuropathy (อ่อนแรง มีหรือไม่มีอาการชาก็ได้ ขึ้นกับสาเหตุที่เป็น)
│   ├── Hereditary: Charcot-Marie-Tooth
│   ├── Demyelination: CIDP, GBS
│   ├── Metabolic: DM* (common), Hypothyroid, Renal failure
│   ├── Drug/toxin: Drug (INH, ETB, Colchicine, Hydralazine, Phenytoin, Paclitaxel), Toxin (Alcohol, Lead, Arsenic)
│   ├── Infection: HIV, HBV, HCV
│   └── Other: Nutritional def, Paraneoplastic, Vasculitis, Amyloidosis, Monoclonal gammopathy
└── Myopathy (อ่อนแรง ร่วมกับมีกล้ามเนื้อลีบ ไม่มีอาการชา มักเป็นโรคทางพันธุกรรม)
    ├── Myotonic dystrophy
    ├── Distal myopathy: Early adult onset (Type 1 Walander, Type 2 Markesbery-Griggs, Type 3), Late adult onset (Type 1 Nonaka, Type 2 Miyoshi, Type 3 Laing)
    ├── Nemaline myopathy
    └── Metabolic disorder: Phosphorylase B kinase def, Debrancher def
```

### 9.5.6 Approach to Quadriparesis
```
Quadriparesis
├── UMN lesion
│   ├── Cerebrum: Lesion bilateral (Bilateral Basal ganglion, Bilateral Cortex) → Recurrence stroke
│   ├── Brainstem: Vascular (Locked-in syndrome/Pontine lesion), Infection (Brainstem abscess, Brainstem encephalitis), Tumor (Glioma), Multiple sclerosis
│   └── Spinal cord: Lesion cervical level → ดูต่อ Spinal cord disorder
└── LMN lesion
    ├── Polyneuropathy: ดูต่อ Distal muscle weakness
    └── Myopathy: ดูต่อ Proximal muscle weakness
```

### 9.5.7 Approach to Wrist Drop
```
Wrist drop → Check muscles sequentially:
1. Brachioradialis Normal → PIN lesion (Posterior interosseous n.)
2. Brachioradialis Abnormal + Tricep Normal → Radial n. at spiral grove (Saturday night palsy)
3. Tricep Abnormal + Deltoid Normal → Radial n. at axilla
4. Deltoid Abnormal → Posterior cord of brachial plexus
```

### 9.5.8 Approach to Foot Drop
```
Foot drop
├── Upper motor neuron: Muscle Spastic, Reflex Hyperreflexia/Clonus, Babinski Present
│   ├── Cerebral cortex: Parasagittal lesion (ACA infarction, Meningioma)
│   └── Spinal cord → ดูต่อเรื่อง "Spinal cord disorder"
└── Lower motor neuron: Muscle Flaccid/Atrophy, Reflex Hyporeflexia, Babinski Absent
    ├── Unilateral → Check muscles:
    │   ├── Gluteus (hip extension/abduction): L5 Plexus, Proximal Sciatic n. lesion
    │   ├── Hamstring (Knee flexion): Distal Sciatic n. lesion
    │   ├── Plantar flexor (เดินปลายเท้า): Common peroneal n. lesion → Yes → Superficial peroneal n. lesion
    │   └── Foot eversion: ถ้าปกติ ดูตำแหน่ง → Yes → Common peroneal n. lesion
    └── Bilateral → Bowel disorder involvement → Yes: Cauda equina lesion; No: Peripheral neuropathy → ดู Distal m weakness
```

## 9.6 Stepwise Approach to Spinal Cord Disorder

### DDx Algorithm:
```
Step 1: Localize level by Hx, Motor, Reflex, Dermatome
  Root: C5 (Deltoid, Bicep reflex), C6 (Bicep), C7 (Triceps, One-finger extension), C8 (Finger extension, Tricep reflex)
  Root: L4 (Quadriceps, Knee reflex), L5 (Great toe extension), S1 (Plantar flexion, Ankle reflex)

Step 2: Extramedullary vs Intramedullary lesion
| Feature           | Intramedullary    | Extramedullary      |
| Vertebral & Radicular pain | Unusual    | Common              |
| UMN signs         | Late              | Early               |
| LMN signs         | Prominent         | Unusual             |
| Paresthesia progression | Descending  | Ascending           |
| Sphincter involvement | Early          | Late                |

Step 3: Determine etiology
| Category    | Intramedullary                     | Extramedullary              |
| Infection   | Infectious myelitis (HIV, HTLV, Herpes), Intraductal abscess | Epidural abscess, TB, Pott disease |
| Vascular    | Spinal infarction                  | AV malformation             |
| Demyelination | MS, NMO, ADEM                   |                              |
| Inflammation | SLE, APS, SS, Post infectious/vaccine |                        |
| Developmental/Degenerative | Syringomyelia | Spondylosis, Herniated disc |

Cord Syndromes:
| Syndrome         | Clinical Signs                                          | Etiology                                    |
| Transection      | Loss of all motor, sensory, and autonomic below lesion  | Trauma, severe compression, necrotizing myelitis |
| Anterior cord    | Bilateral flaccid weakness, Pain/Temperature sensory loss, Autonomic dysfunction | Anterior spinal artery occlusion |
| Dorsal column    | Bilateral reduced vibration, proprioception, touch      | Tabes dorsalis, some MS or B12 deficiency   |
| Brown-Sequard    | Ipsilateral pyramidal weakness + vibration/proprioception loss; contralateral pain loss | Multiple sclerosis, Compression |
| Central          | "Dissociated" sensory loss, Pain/temperature loss with spared vibration/proprioception | Syrinx, neuromyelitis optica |
```

## 9.7 Stepwise Approach to Peripheral Neuropathy

### DDx Algorithm:
```
Peripheral neuropathy: weakness predominant
├── Symmetrical weakness
│   ├── Proximal+Distal weakness
│   │   ├── With Sensory loss: GBS, CIDP
│   │   └── Without Sensory loss: SMA, Spinal muscular atrophy
│   └── Distal weakness
│       ├── C Sensory loss → Hereditary CMT, Metabolic DM, Drug/Toxin INH/Lead, Infection HIV, Idiopathic
│       │   Other: Vasculitis, amyloidosis, Paraneoplastic synd, Monoclonal gammopathy
│       └── w/o Sensory loss → ดู Subtopic: Atypical CMT, Lead neuropathy
└── Asymmetrical weakness
    ├── Proximal+Distal weakness → Polyradiculopathy: DM, Meningeal carcinomatosis, Hereditary plexopathy, Idiopathic
    └── Distal weakness
        ├── w/o Sensory loss → Multiple nerve: Multifocal CIDP, Vasculitis/Cryoglobulinemia, Infiltration (Amyloidosis), Tumor (Sarcoidosis), Infection (HIV, HCV, HBV), Hereditary HNPP
        │   ├── Without UMN signs → Progressive muscular atrophy, Juvenile monomelic atrophy (Hirayama disease), Multifocal motor neuropathy
        │   └── With UMN signs → Motor neuron disease
        └── c Sensory loss → Single nerve: Common peroneal n. lesion (ถ้าเป็นที่ขา), มักเป็น entrapment/compression

Sensory impairment predominant:
├── Symmetrical sensory impairment
│   ├── +/- Distal weakness: Hereditary CMT, Metabolic DM, Drug/Toxin INH/Lead, Infection HIV, Idiopathic, Other (Vasculitis, amyloidosis, Paraneoplastic, Monoclonal gammopathy)
│   ├── Areflexia + UMN: Nutrition (B12/vitE def, Copper def), Combined system degeneration c peripheral neuropathy, Hereditary leukodystrophies
│   └── w/o weakness → Sensory ganglinopathy: Paraneoplastic, Sjogren syndrome, B6 toxicity/Cisplatin, HIV-related, Idiopathic sensory neuronopathy
└── Autonomic symptoms predominant: Hereditary (Hereditary sensory and autonomic neuropathy, Familial amyloidosis), DM, Idiopathic pandysautonomia, Other (Porphyria, HIV, Vincristine)
```

## 9.8 Movement Disorder

### 9.8.1 Classification
```
Movement disorder
├── Hyperkinetic (Rhythmic vs Non-Rhythmic, Voluntary vs Involuntary)
│   ├── Rhythmic → Tremor
│   └── Non-Rhythmic → Mnemonic "ABCD-MT"
│       A: Athetosis (Involuntary, Slow, Writhing movement เด่นที่ distal limb)
│       B: Ballism (Proximal limb)
│       C: Chorea (Distal limb)
│       D: Dystonia (Sustain muscle contraction)
│       M: Myoclonus (Rapid Jerk, No premonitory urge)
│       T: Tic (Premonitory urge)
└── Hypokinetic → Parkinsonism
```

### 9.8.2 Tremor
```
Tremor (Rhythmic, involuntary oscillatory movement of body part)
├── Resting: Parkinson disease (PD), Wilson disease
├── Postural: Essential tremor (ET), Physiologic tremor, Anxiety/fear, Sleep deprivation, Alcohol/Drug withdrawal, Thyrotoxicosis, Wilson disease
└── Intention: Cerebellar disease, Drug and toxin, Wilson disease

| Feature              | Parkinson disease | Essential Tremor         |
| Type tremor          | Resting tremor    | Action (Postural, kinetic) |
| Family history       | Less common       | Common                    |
| Unilateral or bilateral | เริ่มข้างเดียวก่อน (Unilateral) | ทั้งสองข้าง (Bilateral) |
| Response to treatment | Responds Levidopa | Responds Alcohol, Beta-blocker, Neurontin |

Common cause of secondary Tremor: Alcohol, AED withdrawal, Cerebellar disease, Valproic acid, Thiazide, B-agonist/amphetamine/cocaine/caffeine
```

### 9.8.3 Chorea
```
Chorea (Quick, irregular, involuntary, semi or non purposive, Predominant distal movement)
├── Primary "Hereditary": AD (Huntington, SCA), AR (Wilson, Neuroacanthocytosis), Mitochondrial disease
└── Secondary
    ├── Extracranial
    │   ├── Metabolic: Hypo/hyperglycemia, Hyperthyroid, HypoPTH/Ca low, Mg low/Na high/O2 low
    │   ├── Drug: Neuroleptic, Anticonvulsant, Antiparkinson
    │   └── Toxin: CO, Mn
    └── Intracranial → Basal ganglion lesion
        ├── Vascular: Vasculitis
        ├── Tumor
        ├── Autoimmune: SLE, Antiphospholipid, Sydenham
        ├── Infection: Encephalitis, AIDS/CJD, PANDAS
        └── Other: Cerebral palsy, Pregnancy
```

### 9.8.4 Myoclonus
```
Myoclonus (Sudden, brief, shock-like jerks)
├── Exclude: Physiologic myoclonus (Hypnic jerk, Hiccup, Physiologic startle)
├── Exclude: Psychogenic myoclonus
├── Essential myoclonus (ไม่มี epilepsy): Sporadic, Familial (no gene identified), Myoclonic dystonia (Gene: GYTE or DYT11)
├── Epileptic myoclonus (มีไม่มี epilepsy): ดูรายละเอียด แล้วจัดว่าเป็น Myoclonus
└── Symptomatic or Secondary Myoclonus (มีโรคอะไรก่อ ที่ทำให้เป็น Myoclonus)
    ├── Neuro: Posthypoxic myoclonus (Lance-Adams syndrome), Neurodegenerative dz (Wilson, PSP, MSA), Spinocerebellar Ataxia (Ramsay-Hunt, Friedreich, Ataxia-telangiectasia), Dementia (PD c dementia, Dementia c lewy bodies)
    ├── Autoimmune: Limbic encephalitis, Hashimoto's encephalopathy, Creutzfeldt-Jakob (Economiglue)
    ├── Storage: Gaucher's dz, Tay-Sachs dz, Lafora body dz
    ├── Metabolic/Endocrine: Glucose high/low, Thyrotoxicosis, Renal failure, Electrolyte (Na/Ca/Mg low), Liver failure, Mitochondrial dysfunction
    ├── ID/Prion/ID: CJD, Herpes encephalitis, HIV/HCV/H1, Malaria, By Subacute sclerosing panencephalitis
    ├── Toxin: Alcohol, Aluminum
    ├── Drug: Levodopa, TCA/SSRI, BZD
    └── Paraneoplastic
```

## 9.9 Visual Disorder

### 9.9.1 Blurred Vision and Visual Acuity Loss
```
Blurred vision → Clarify patient symptoms:
├── Visual field defect → ดูต่อ Visual field defect
├── Visual acuity defect → Anatomical approach
│   ├── Ocular disorder
│   │   ├── Anterior segment: Cornea (Corneal ulcer), Anterior chamber (Glaucoma), Lens (Cataract)
│   │   └── Posterior segment: Vitreous (Vitreous hemorrhage), Retina (Papilledema, Retinal detachment, Retinal hemorrhage), Retinal vascular (CRAO, CRVO, Hypoperfusion, Embolic, DM, EP, Hyperviscosity)
│   └── Neurologic disorder: Optic n. lesion (Optic neuritis, AION, PION), Occipital lobe lesion (Cortical, Stroke, Tumor)
├── Diplopia → ดูต่อ Diplopia
└── Oscillopsia → Cerebellar lesion, Brain stem lesion

Time-based approach to Visual acuity loss:
├── Sudden visual loss → Transient (การสูญเสียการมองเห็นชั่วคราว)
│   ├── Monocular: Blood supply (I.to retina CVD, Atherosclerotic fugax), Hypoperfusion, BP, Hemodynamically, Vascular disease, Vasospasm
│   └── Binocular: Other (Migraine, AACG, Improving CRVO)
├── Sudden + Without progression
│   ├── Monocular: Vascular dz (Central hypoperfusion, PRES, Seizure, AION, Retina dz (Retinal detachment, Serious retinopathy), Migraine
│   └── Binocular: Vascular dz (CRAO, CRVO, PION), Start CVI (Pituitary apoplexy, Ischemic stroke, Head trauma)
└── Progressive visual loss → Art visual pathway inflammation (Optic neuritis, Sarcoidosis, meningitis, Radiation), Compression (Tumor, Aneurysm, Pre-retinal edema retinopathy), Glaucoma, Chronic papilledema, Toxic (Ethambutol), Paraneoplastic retinopathy
```

### 9.9.2 Diplopia
```
Diplopia
├── กฎ สำคัญ: 1) Monocular vs Binocular, 2) Vertical vs Horizontal, 3) Directionality (ปิดตาข้างไหนแล้วหาย?), 4) EOM Full/Vergence?, 5) Binocular Pursuit eye movement, 6) Face/Ocular abnormality, 7) Oculocephalic maneuver

├── Monocular Diplopia (ปิดตาแย้มเดียวแล้วยังเห็นภาพคู่)
│   ├── Ophthalmic: Macula abnormality
│   ├── Neurologic: Occipital lobe lesion
│   └── Drug induce: visual hallucination
└── Binocular Diplopia (มองด้วยตาสองข้างไม่ตรงแกนตา แล้วเห็นภาพไม่ตรงกัน)
    ├── Functional → เปิดปิดตา/misalignment
    ├── Orbital processing: Orbital floor fracture, Muscle infiltration, Orbital myositis/Orbital pseudotumor, Thyroid-associated ophthalmopathy
    ├── Congenital strabismus: Esotropia, Exotropia
    ├── Neurologic disorder
    │   ├── Supranuclear: INO
    │   ├── Cranial nerve: Isolated CN (CN III/IV/VI palsy), Multiple CN (Brainstem, Base of skull, Cavernous/SOF)
    │   ├── NMJ: Myasthenia gravis
    │   └── Ocular myopathy: CPEO, Kearns-Sayre, Oculopharyngeal dystrophy
    └── Decompensated strabismus

Common cause:
| Horizontal Diplopia | Vertical Diplopia |
| Supranuclear: INO   | IV palsy, III palsy |
| Cranial nerve: III/VI palsy | NMJ: MG |
| NMJ: MG             | Ocular: Thyroid-associated ophthalmopathy |
| Ocular: Decompensated Strabismus | |

Painful ophthalmoplegia:
| Location          | Causes |
| Orbit             | Tumor, Primary/Metastasis, Orbital pseudotumor, Infection (Sinusitis, Actinomycosis, Mucormycosis) |
| Cavernous sinus   | Tumor, Primary/Metastatic, Inflammation, Tolosa-Hunt syndrome, Vascular (Carotid a. aneurysm, Cavernous sinus thrombosis, AV fistula) |
| Sellar/posterior fossa | Pituitary tumor, Pituitary apoplexy, Metastatic tumor, Aneurysm |
| Other             | Diabetic Ophthalmoplegia, Ophthalmoplegic Migraine, Giant cell arteritis |
```

### 9.9.3 Visual Field Defect
```
Visual field defect (with diagrams showing lesion locations):
1. Central scotoma Rt eye → Lesion: Optic disc/Optic nerve Rt → Optic neuritis/Retrobulbar neuritis
2. Total blindness of Rt eye → Complete lesion of optic nerve → ดูสาเหตุที่ Visual acuity loss

7. Left homonymous inferior quadrantanopia → Partial involvement of optic radiation → Lesion in right parietal lobe
8. Left homonymous hemianopia with macular sparing → Occipital lobe lesion → PCA infarction
```
